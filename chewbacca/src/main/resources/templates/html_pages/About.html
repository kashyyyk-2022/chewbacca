Routemeister is an application suitable for both mobile and website use, whose purpose is to generate a walking route based on the user’s preferences. These preferences include length, time, elevation, terrain, and availability for wheelchairs and strollers. The idea behind Routemeister is to let users find new routes and explore new parts of a city that would otherwise go unexplored.

This application is part of a project course in agile software programming done at Chalmers university, where the scope was to make an application that is somehow connected to the United Nations sustainable development goals . This project is centred around goal 3.4, reduce mortality from non-communicable diseases and promote mental health, and goal 11.7, provide access to safe and inclusive green and public spaces.

The people involved in this project is Wincent Holm, Rasmus Standar, Oscar Marrero Engström, Viktoria Hagenbo, Isak Holmdahl, Marcus Phu, and Lovisa Rosin.
